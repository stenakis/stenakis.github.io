Aegina in a (pistachio) nutshell

Aegina Fistiki Fest is a lively annual celebration held on the Greek island of Aegina, dedicated to the island’s famous pistachios — known locally as "fistikia". Every September, the main port fill with colorful stalls selling everything from fresh and roasted pistachios to sweets, liqueurs, and artisan products made with it. Alongside the market, the festival features concerts, art exhibitions and traditional dance performances, creating a festive atmosphere that blends local culture, gastronomy, and island hospitality.

My role

As an Art Director, I was the one who pushed this project to the direction of showing of the locality of Pistachio. The slogan “Fistiki apo ton topo tou” which briefly translates to “Pistachio from its place of origin” - or “Because origin matters” as suggested by the client - really highlights the goal of the festival.

“Fistiki Fest is the most famous event on the island. Together with Evans, we wanted to give a special treat to our beloved friend, the Pistachio.” Fotini from YellowFish

The protagonists

“Church of Agios Nikolaos”
The small church of Agios Nikolaos with its quicklime white color and blue details is the ultimate Greek island element.

“Temple of Afaia”
One of the most famous ancient Greek temples of the island with thousands of visitors each year.

“Markellos Tower”
Its color is all around the island. One of the oldest houses (now used as a museum) of the island.

“Palaiachora”
The old main town of Aegina, protected from pirates with hundred of small churches and an immense history.
